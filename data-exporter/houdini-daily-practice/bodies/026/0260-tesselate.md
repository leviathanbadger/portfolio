### Tesselate

This is the thirtieth prompt of the [Nodevember 2025](https://www.nodevember-2025.com/),
"Tesselate".

I'll return to the 2024 prompts later, but since I skipped nodevember this year in
favor of writing 30k words in November, I'm going to go to the 2025 prompts and do
the 2024 prompts later.

It's meant to be prison courtyard on a foggy night. I spent a lot of effort on
geometric detail for the fence, and a bit of effort on the prison itself in the
background, but 1. it's too dark to make out most of it; and 2. there actually still
isn't enough detail for it to be recognizably a prison. In retrospect, I probably
should have gone with a day scene and kept the dreary feel by making it overcast and
washed out. The time I would have saved by fiddling with the fog and lights could
have gone into giant blue trash bins, dumpsters, doors and lit interiors, an elevated
guard station, etc. to make the scene more lived in.

I don't regret doing this, but I admit it's turned out quite a bit worse than I was
hoping. I also spent about ~8 hours on it over the course of three days, which I
think puts this in the top three for time spent on a single project for the Nodevember
2025 prompts, beaten only by the Hotline from [[19. Tool|day 19]], and possibly the
Oldest House from [[12. Monument|day 12]] (I didn't record how long it took). It's
also probably within the top 50% or so in terms of quality, but it's lower than is
realistically should be considering the time and effort that went into it.

Will I return to this project and fix some of the issues with it? Maybe, but probably
not for a while. It's just not exciting me the way it did when I started.

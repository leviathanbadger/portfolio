### Mardini 2026 - Environment

Today is day 5 of [Mardini 2026][mardini-2026]. The prompt for today is "Environment".

This prompt is a freebie, since basically any image could fit it so long as it
portrays a setting instead of a single subject and isn't ridiculously abstract. I'm
honestly not sure how I settled on 'rice paddies' as the selection, but I did, and
here we are. I don't like how it turned out, for several reasons.

1. I have little to no experience with grass/plant materials with subsurface
   scattering.
2. I feel like I messed up the scale on several components, which made it very hard
   to reason about camera positioning and material parameters.
3. Also, the water material, while fine on paper, looks pretty awful because it
   doesn't have floating moss/mud or anything to ground it in the scene.
4. The grass and rice don't have enough variation (in height, color, placement, etc.)
   and there's no attempt at macrovariation.
5. The terraces are too uniform.
6. There's nothing in the scene to indicate scale. (Buildings, trees, people.) The
   camera could be ten feet off the ground or two.
7. The dirt texture I threw together looks like brown clay or something.

So altogether the image turns into an overly abstract piece that I'm fairly confident
nobody will recognize to be depicting rice paddies. Despite that, somehow it doesn't
look awful; it just doesn't look like... anything in particular.

New things:

- HeightField tools, including HeightField Terrace SOP.
- Point instancing with variants. (And >1000000 points to instance to.)
- Vellum simulation for grass and rice.
- Subsurface scattering for grass/rice material.
